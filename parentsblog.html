How Can You Embrace Your Inner Sandwich?

By Our Grandparents & Us founder Victoria H. MAR. 1, 2020

As our health care options improve and expand, our families are growing in number and in age. Parents are now beginning to find themselves "sandwiched" between two generations- or are they really getting torn apart?

Caregiving is a difficult job, and looking out for rowdy, talkative kids is tough already. But when we add opinionated, confused grandparents into the mix, a parent's focus, life, and place in a person's world becomes all the more unclear.

Who's The Priority?

"...I read about the term 'sandwich generation.' I remember thinking it was a clever concept, but it still didnâ€™t occur to me that I was a member of this group. I was merely a caregiver and a mom."

- Carol Bradley Bursack, author of "Minding Our Elders: Caregivers Share Their Personal Stories"

As a kid, it was difficult for me to wrap my mind around the idea of taking care of two totally different age groups all at once, so I questioned my mom for her perspective. What exactly does keeping watch over two opposite generations look like?

A person in the sandwich generation is tasked with meeting varied and vastly different needs, whether it's taking the kids to music practice or wheeling Grandma to her best friend's funeral. Although that's a problem, my mom said, she suggested gathering up the whole family and brainstorming an effective schedule together. But as much of an effective schedule your family makes, there is always family emergencies or sudden events to be dealt with. It can be a hassle to find a way to balance everyone's needs, but with lots of love and effort, any parent can do it. Difficult decisions are something everyone in the sandwich generation has to deal with, and it's necessary to embrace the positive impact of your choices and remember how lucky you are to have both your parents and your kids!

Caregiving and Careers

My mom and dad, like many other parents, have work and jobs to worry about- along with their parents, my little brother, and me. For many parents, being a caregiver is a non-stop anxiety rush. At work, you can't stop thinking about your kids and how they're doing their homework. At home, you can't stop thinking about your parents, if they're okay and if you forgot something they needed. With your parents, you can't stop thinking about if you neglected your job. Stop worrying! Take a moment to relax each day, and hold on to the trust you have in your family. Let them know how you're feeling. Send them a quick checkup text if that ache in your heart won't go away. Be present in each moment- focus on what you're doing. Then, you'll do a great job, no matter what it is you're doing.

Asking For Help

"Families are like branches on a tree. We grow in different directions but our roots remain as one."

- Unknown

One of the most important things to remember as a parent is that you're not alone. It can be easy to take it the wrong way and summon defenses whenever somebody mentions that there are millions of people going through the same problems you are. It's strange to react that way, especially since we definitely should know better. If there are millions of people here to reach out to, then REACH OUT! This is the gift the Our Grandparents & Us website has given you. The ability to connect with many parents is something so valuable, and now it's something you have. Ask questions, and answer them. Embrace your inner sandwich, and let others have a bite of your goodness.
